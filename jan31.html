Jan 31

Jan 31 Blog

Tomorrow’s the big day, and suddenly I’m feeling very nervous. I repacked my bag, and it’s now 28 pounds – and I learned how to carry it on my back if worst comes to worst. It looks slightly awkward (hehe, also I was cranky from just waking up – and look at me being so stylish with my oversized hoodie that’s my brothers and my pants that are my fathers haha), but it’s much easier than carrying it with one hand (although I get shoulder burn ) I still have to do a lot of readings to do for classes, but I’m hoping I can read through some of it on my plane ride to California since I usually can’t fall asleep. I bought a travel pillow though! I’ve bought so many things in the past two weeks (Look at all the boxes! And that is just HALF of all the stuff I bought). Part of me is worried that I overpacked, but there’s not really anything I can do about it at this point. I’m bringing duct tape with me just in case my rolling duffel bag starts falling apart – which it might, and is a big fear of mine (I can’t count the number of reviews I’ve read on rolling duffel bags and suitcases in general – but it seems like either some of people have no problem, or it falls apart – but there’s not a solid consensus, so it just seems like luck too, please keep my luggage in your thoughts haha) – but hopefully all will be well  I’ve just been scrambling trying to do last minute things. I decided I needed a simple dress, so I went to six different stores trying on clothes – Tip: It’s generally not a good idea to shop for spring time dresses in the winter :P, but it helped to get out of the house for a bit. I also found another shirt that has a cool pattern in the closet that I think I’ll take with me too. I still feel like there’s so much to do, but I’m just reminding myself that I still have two weeks in California before I actually leave the country. My dad has been giving me non-stop advice on how to not get sick haha: “don’t eat anything cold” “don’t eat anything that’s not cooked” “boil your water”, etc. etc., so that’s been a fun time. We went to a cool Mexican restaurant though that had a really cool interior design cause of the tile work, and it reminded me of some the photos I’ve seen of Morocco. I think I’m still in shock that the day has finally come to leave home. Up till this point, it’s just been an abstract thing in the future, but now it’s turning into a real thing, and I’m actually doing it. It’s definitely going out of my comfort zone, and I’m taking a big leap, but that’s also why I decided to do this program.

The journey begins in the morning.
