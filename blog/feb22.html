Contradictions

The world is filled with contradictions, and with this study abroad – I’m facing it up close. While I was overwhelmed with the kindness I have found in this country, this past weekend has showed another side to one of my friends that I have become close to on this trip. She is an African American, and she has gotten a lot of unwanted attention here. I’m assuming that some of these people have never seen a black person in order to stare, point, take pictures, and touch her without her permission. It is hard to grapple with – but something that is so prevalent throughout the world. I wish I could say that this is an isolated incident, but if I were being honest with myself, I am not sure if the reactions of people in Nepal would be much different. When encountered with something unfamiliar, it is hard to know how people will react. Everyone’s experience on this program will be shaped individually even though we are going through this as a group. That’s something that I’ve been thinking about a lot recently. While I am usually the one to be sociable, I find myself becoming more silent and wanting to observe on this trip. I’ve found so much value in silence and having alone time with your thoughts since we are constantly with each other. I’ve become more of a loner in this sense – but I am okay with that. I no longer feel the need to always talk, and I instead want to listen or just to sit and observe.

The world is so good, but bad at the same time. There’s so many problems with society, but if we tear down the whole system then I don’t know what’s left. This past weekend I was in Ha Long Bay, and it was one of the most serene places I have been. I just sat on the boat and looked ahead at the beauty in front of me. However, environmental degradation was very apparent. The corals that I was kayaking near were dead, there was trash in the water, and tourism had highly impacted the place in a negative way. There’s a contradiction here too – while tourism has negatively impacted the environment, it has probably brought a lot of economic growth to this region. A lot of people make their livelihoods off showing people this natural wonder. Similarly, with environmental problems in the United States, there a lot of contradictions as well. Some of it stems from the fact that we live in capitalistic society, which is weird to say being an economics major where classes constantly talk about the positives of a free market. But there are so many divisions that the system creates and perpetuates – but then again, if we don’t have that system, then what’s the alternative? I have so many questions, but no satisfactory answer.
