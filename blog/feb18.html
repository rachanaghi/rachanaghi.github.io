Feb 18

“Honesty about your struggle is the key to liberation” ~ Yolo Akili

My time in Vietnam thus far has been great. However, I am struggling physically and mentally at times with the feeling of exhaustion. I hope I continue to be thankful and realize this is not an opportunity that comes frequently. While there are challenges in this program, I will learn and grow throughout it. I will be honest with myself about my struggles in order to be liberated as the quote suggests. I have realized that the world is filled with kindness at a time when I was starting to lose faith. The smiles from the people at the hotel, or the street, or at restaurants remind me of how much good there is in the world. Juggling academics with trying to discover a country is not easy. I’m constantly trying to find a balance in this, but I have learned so much through both of these things that it is hard to complain. While the language barrier has been tough at times, it has also helped form a connection on a more basic level. We all have one thing in common: we are all human. While we have to make gestures at each other to get our points across, it has helped foster a want to understand each other that reconnects us.
